My Experience

Over the period of 6 months, I worked with Priyal (the founder of sprimate studios and leader of this project) as well as three fellow student interns to create a board game from scratch. While the three other students were artists, it was up to me to spearhead everything that went into the gameplay. That meant I worked closely with Priyal to fine tune rules, mechanics, and come up with/refine character abilities & card effects.

The goal was to finalize a playable and entertaining first prototype so that student interns in the following cycle can help perfect the project to make it ready for shipping. In the end not only did we achieve our goal, but we were able to playtest the game at public events (resulting in lots of positive feedback), in addition to starting potential expansion packs if the game were to gain a bigger audience.

It was here where I created three potential themes each with 5 unique heroes.

  • The cryptid pack contained 5 different Cryptids with the core mechanic of gaining powerful stats & abilities (inspired by their real stories) once they are in a "wounded" state. Meaning they are more powerful the closer to death.
  • The gods & goddesses pack contained 5 gods, each from different mythologies around the world with a core mechanic of affecting various game mechanics like the draw pile, environmental hazards, token collection, etc.
  • Then my final pack was the kaiju pack where it contained 5 beloved and powerful kaiju. Their main mechanic came from having incredibly high stats (with the exception of speed in which they were very slow) but not only just 1 direction to attack, but also no special abilities. Meaning with the right setup they could be completely overwhelmed with almost no ways to escape.
