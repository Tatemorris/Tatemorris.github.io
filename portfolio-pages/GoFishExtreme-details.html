My Experience

For our first assignment in the class, we were tasked with creating an extension to a pre-existing tabletop game within 2 weeks. It was my role to serve as a primary game designer for the project, working to help create the rules of our game extension, and after deciding we wanted to expand Go Fish!, come up with ability cards that would add new layers of strategy in addition to more interactions with players and the drawdeck.

We did extensive playtests and in the end I am incredibly proud of what we created. We were able to really turn Go Fish! into a really fun game that I could see modern audiences enjoying!

So, these are the adjustments I personally worked on:

  • The Siren card: One of the 3 special cards I created, it's description reads: The beautiful tune of the sirenâ€™s voice lures fishermen to want to hear your offers! Instead of asking one person for a card, you now ask everyone. If no one has the cards you are looking for, then draw cards equal to the amount of players and end your turn.
  • The Fishing Rod card: The second card I worked on, it's description reads: A rod with a special lure helps you catch unseen fish! Keep drawing from the draw deck until you draw a basic fish. Then shuffle all other cards you just drew back into the deck.
  • The Sonar card: The last card I worked on, its description reads: This military grade sonar tracker can give you the intel you need! Look at the top 3 cards from the deck and put them back in the same order.
  • The Rules: I was in charge with modifying the original rules of Go Fish! to fit our expansion. So I came up with the rules document shown in the first slide of this webpage which you can see has a table determining starting draw size, explaination of differences in what card you ask for each turn/why, clarification on previously state rules, etc. The background of the doc was created by one of the artists on our team, Charlie.
