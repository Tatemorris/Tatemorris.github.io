Project Details

 Enter Christina Lyon, a death row inmate falsely accused of the murder of her husband and daughter. She is taken to a prison run by Charybdis Industries, a corporation secretly experimenting on an amphibious carnivore in a secure laboratory within the same facility. When the subject escapes and goes on a killing spree, Christina wakes to find herself the sole survivor.

 Now trapped in an abandoned facility with a dangerous creature, Christina must find a way to escape, using the subject’s attraction to noise to break down walls. Along the way, she will reminisce about the facility’s dead inhabitants and uncover exactly why the creature was there in the first place.

 As the project’s Technical Designer and Lead Designer, I designed and implemented the game’s core mechanic from prototype through continued development, ensuring the system remained readable, tunable, and scalable for the team. The central design challenge is deliberate: the monster is both a tool and a threat. Players must create sound to influence its behavior and direct it toward destructible walls, but any misstep can trigger aggression, a killing swipe, or an overwhelming screech. This creates a tense, systemic relationship where progression requires calculated risk, communication, and controlled provocation.

 My work centered on building the monster-facing gameplay systems that make this loop possible. I designed the monster’s AI behavior set, its actions, perception responses, escalation logic, and scream behavior. All of this implemented in Unreal Engine 5 using parameterized, designer-friendly logic so that other designers and programmers can quickly tune thresholds, timings, ranges, and reactions to match pacing and difficulty targets. In parallel, I designed and implemented a Sanity mechanic to support the narrative and psychological horror tone, using systemic changes to player perception and moment-to-moment tension as a storytelling device rather than relying solely on cutscenes.

 Beyond the core loop, I implemented supporting stealth and interaction systems, including player hiding, modular object triggers, and additional progression-facing mechanics that enable encounter scripting and reliable scenario flow. I documented each mechanic with clear intent, dependencies, tunable parameters, and edge cases to improve handoff and iteration speed across disciplines. I also presented the project during Champlain’s Greenlight presentations, organized and hosted playtests, and incorporated feedback into ongoing revisions and balance passes. All work that led to the team nominating me as Lead Designer for the project.
